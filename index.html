About the Doctor

Kevin Saladino, MD

Dr. Kevin Saladino is a board-certified psychiatrist dedicated to providing exceptional, evidence-based psychiatric care. With a deep commitment to the doctor-patient relationship, Dr. Saladino brings both clinical expertise and genuine compassion to every encounter.

His practice is built on the belief that meaningful, lasting improvement comes from truly understanding each patient's unique story — not simply managing symptoms. Dr. Saladino partners with his patients to develop treatment plans that reflect their values, goals, and circumstances.

Medical Degree — University of Illinois at Chicago
Residency — The Ohio State University, Psychiatry
Member, American Psychiatric Association

Clinical Services

Specialized Psychiatric Care

Dr. Saladino offers a range of services designed to address the full spectrum of adult psychiatric needs, from initial evaluation through ongoing care.

Initial Psychiatric Evaluation

A comprehensive, unhurried assessment to understand your mental health history, current concerns, and treatment goals. The foundation of all care.

Medication Management

Thoughtful, evidence-based prescribing and ongoing medication management tailored to your individual neurobiology and treatment response.

Psychotherapy

Individual therapy using evidence-based modalities including cognitive-behavioral, psychodynamic, and supportive approaches, matched to your needs.

Ongoing Psychiatric Care

Consistent follow-up visits to monitor progress, refine treatment, and provide continuity of care as your life and needs evolve over time.

Dr. Saladino has experience treating a broad range of conditions including depression, anxiety disorders, bipolar disorder, ADHD, trauma and PTSD, OCD, and adjustment difficulties. All services are available both in-person and via telehealth.

Getting Started

What to Expect

Seeking psychiatric care for the first time can feel uncertain. Here is a simple overview of what the process looks like with Dr. Saladino.

01

Reach Out

Send a message through the contact form or call the office. You will hear back to confirm availability and answer any initial questions.

02

Initial Evaluation

Your first appointment is a 60-minute conversation. Dr. Saladino will take time to understand your history, your concerns, and what you're hoping to achieve.

03

Your Treatment Plan

Together, you and Dr. Saladino will discuss a personalized plan — which may include therapy, medication, or a combination — that fits your needs and goals.

04

Ongoing Care

Follow-up visits allow Dr. Saladino to monitor your progress, adjust your plan as needed, and provide consistent support over time.

You do not need to have everything figured out before reaching out. The first step is simply starting the conversation.

Payment & Fees

Self-Pay Practice

Dr. Saladino operates as a private, self-pay practice. This model allows for greater flexibility, privacy, and a care relationship focused entirely on you — free from the constraints of insurance.

Upon request, a superbill can be provided after each visit, which you may submit to your insurance company for potential out-of-network reimbursement. Many patients with PPO plans receive partial reimbursement this way.

Legal Notice

No Surprises Act — Good Faith Estimate

Under the federal No Surprises Act, patients who do not have insurance or who are not using insurance to pay for their care have the right to receive a Good Faith Estimate of the expected charges for medical services.

You have the right to receive a Good Faith Estimate for the total expected cost of any non-emergency services, including psychiatric evaluation and treatment, before you schedule an appointment. If you receive a bill that is at least $400 more than your Good Faith Estimate, you have the right to dispute the charges through a federal patient-provider dispute resolution process.

You may request a Good Faith Estimate from our office at any time before scheduling services or during treatment if additional services are recommended.

For questions or more information about your rights under the No Surprises Act, visit cms.gov/nosurprises or call 1-800-985-3059.
